How to use this competency framework template

Many organizations struggle with developing detailed competency frameworks that are actionable and broad enough to apply across departments.

By following these steps, your organization can develop a robust and effective competency framework that supports talent development and organizational success. This structured approach ensures that competencies are relevant, measurable, and aligned with strategic objectives.

Steps to follow:

  • Begin by identifying a set of core competencies that all employees should possess—this ensures a unified baseline of skills and behaviors across the organization (e.g., decision-making, communication, emotional intelligence)
  • Involve internal stakeholders from each department to identify relevant technical and functional competencies—this step ensures that the selected competencies cater to the specific needs and requirements of each department (e.g., creating performance appraisal systems, and designing retention strategies for HR professionals, SEO, and copywriting for marketing departments)
  • Develop detailed competency models for each department or role— Adjust the competency mix based on the nature of your organization; for instance, allocate more technical competencies for highly technical roles and emphasize leadership competencies if you are developing a leadership pipeline or have many managerial roles
  • Limit the number of competencies—Maintain focus by limiting the total number of core, functional, and technical competencies to a maximum of 12 per role
  • Include leadership competencies for senior roles—For senior levels and leadership roles, include leadership competencies, but keep the total number of competencies to a maximum of 15
  • Assign levels of mastery for all core, functional, technical, and leadership competencies—Define levels of mastery (e.g., basic, intermediate, competent, advanced) for each competency according to the specific roles within the organization
  • Customize performance metrics—If relevant to your talent management system, customize the performance metrics for each level according to your organizational objectives to ensure that performance assessments are meaningful and aligned with business goals

Remember, the quality and relevance of the competencies are more critical than the quantity. Ensuring that each competency is clearly defined and aligns with the organization's goals and values is essential.
