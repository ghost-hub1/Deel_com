Does your remote team deal with proprietary information, trade secrets, and intellectual property that would be damaging or costly if leaked? If so, It’s time to consider implementing a confidentiality agreement.

Remote workers may store and share company information on personal devices, connect to public networks and perform their duties in public settings. These activities increase the risk of confidential information being disclosed to competitors or the general public.

Confidentiality agreement template overview

A confidentiality agreement, also called a confidential disclosure agreement (CDA) or nondisclosure agreement (NDA), is a binding legal contract between a company and a remote worker. It ensures the remote worker does not disclose confidential business information to third parties without authorization.

The policy includes:

  • Confidentiality agreement overview
  • Important definitions to note
  • The remote worker’s obligations
  • Exceptions to the agreement
  • Length of the agreement
  • Remote worker acknowledgments

How to use this template

This template is a customizable document. You’ll see placeholder text and prompts to guide your agreement to reflect your local labor laws and your team’s unique approach to remote work.

We suggest integrating the signing of this agreement in your remote workers’ onboarding process to ensure they review and understand the details of the agreement before they commence work.
