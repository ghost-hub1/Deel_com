Leadership competency framework template overview

Leadership is critical to any successful organization, yet developing leaders can be challenging and time-consuming. As a result, it is crucial to have a comprehensive and inspirational framework to guide your effective leadership development efforts.

Our leadership framework template is the result of extensive research and input from leading organizations such as The Executive Coaching Forum, OECD, and ICC (International Criminal Court), as well as academic resources such as S. Sanghi's Handbook of Competency Mapping.

What you'll find in this leadership competency framework template:

  • Definition of each competency: A clear and concise explanation of the competency's purpose and significance
  • Five mastery levels: Competencies span five levels of mastery—from basic to expert—to ensure a clear path for career progression
  • Behavioral indicators: A comprehensive list of observable and measurable behaviors that demonstrate the presence of leadership skills in an individual
  • Performance metrics and KPIs: A set of performance indicators and key performance metrics that reviewers can use to evaluate leaders on the mastery of specific competencies
  • Developmental activities: An extensive list of concrete and actionable steps, training programs, mentoring, and other developmental activities that can help leaders attain expert competency mastery
  • Editable template: Feel free to add additional competencies or proficiency levels or edit the template down with the relevant competencies for your organization

How to use this leadership competency framework template

Follow these steps to create a comprehensive leadership competency framework tailored to your organization’s specific needs:

  1. Select core leadership competencies: Choose core leadership competencies from the database—these should be universally applicable across the organization, ensuring a consistent standard of leadership excellence
  2. Incorporate functional and technical competencies: For a comprehensive framework, add relevant functional and technical competencies specific to different departments to ensure that the framework addresses general leadership qualities and specific departmental needs
  3. Engage stakeholders: Involve key stakeholders from different departments to ensure the selected competencies align with organizational goals and departmental needs—you can use interviews, focus groups, or surveys to collect stakeholder insights
  4. Adjust the distribution of competencies: Adjust the distribution of competencies based on your organization's unique needs and structure
  5. Map competencies to roles: Align the selected competencies with specific roles within your organization to ensure that each role has a clear set of expectations and development goals
  6. Customize and adapt: Tailor the competencies and their descriptions to reflect your organization’s culture and strategic objectives
  7. Limit the total number of competencies: Maintain focus by keeping the total number of competencies to a maximum of 15
  8. Communicate clearly: Develop a communication plan to explain the new competency framework to all employees, highlighting its benefits and how the organization will use it
