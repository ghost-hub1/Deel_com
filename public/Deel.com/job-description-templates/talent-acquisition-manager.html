  • Managing the recruitment and onboarding process
  • Building and maintaining relationships with internal stakeholders and external partners and recruitment agencies

While these figures provide a general indication, consider other factors when assessing Talent Acquisition Manager salaries:

  • Certifications: Holding relevant certifications like PHR or TMP may positively impact salary levels.

How to become a Talent Acquisition Manager

While there is no standardized path to becoming a Talent Acquisition Manager, several common steps can guide individuals aspiring to this role.

Common pathways to becoming a Talent Acquisition Manager

Education 

Attaining formal education in human resources, business administration, or a related field is a common starting point for becoming a Talent Acquisition Manager. Relevant coursework may include recruitment strategies, employment law, and organizational behavior.

Professional experience 

Hands-on experience in talent acquisition and recruitment is crucial for aspiring Talent Acquisition Managers. Many start their careers in entry-level HR roles or recruitment positions, progressively taking on more responsibilities and acquiring leadership skills.

Networking 

Building a professional network within the human resources and recruitment industry can open doors to opportunities and provide valuable insights. Networking with HR professionals, attending industry conferences, and joining HR associations contribute to professional growth.

Talent Acquisition Manager education

Degree 

While there is no specific degree exclusively for talent acquisition, having a solid educational background in human resources, business administration, or a related field is advantageous. Many professionals in this role hold a bachelor's or master's degree in areas such as:

  • Human resources management
  • Business administration
  • Organizational psychology
  • Industrial relations

A background in these fields provides a foundational understanding of HR principles, employment law, and organizational dynamics.

Professional certifications 

Relevant certifications can enhance your credibility and demonstrate your expertise in talent acquisition. Consider certifications from recognized HR organizations, such as the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) or the Human Resource Certification Institute (HRCI). Some relevant certifications include:

  • Professional in Human Resources (PHR)
  • Senior Professional in Human Resources (SPHR)
  • Talent Acquisition Strategist (TAS)

Recruitment and selection training 

Given the central role of recruitment in talent acquisition, specialized training in recruitment and selection processes is valuable. Attend workshops, webinars, or training programs that cover areas such as:

  • Behavioral interviewing techniques
  • Applicant tracking systems (ATS)
  • Diversity and inclusion in hiring

Employment law and compliance 

Talent acquisition managers need a solid understanding of employment laws and regulations to ensure compliance throughout the hiring process. Consider taking courses or workshops on employment law, equal employment opportunity (EEO), and other relevant legal aspects.

Alternative career paths

In some cases, individuals transition into the role of Talent Acquisition Manager from different professional backgrounds. Understanding which roles align well with a transition to talent acquisition can help plan a successful career switch.

Roles that fit well with a transition to Talent Acquisition Manager generally fall into these fields:

Recruitment

Professionals with extensive experience in recruitment often transition into Talent Acquisition Manager roles due to their in-depth knowledge of the recruitment process and industry.

Human resources 

Individuals with a background in broader HR functions, such as HR generalists or specialists, may find their skills transferable to talent acquisition management, especially if they have experience in recruitment.

Corporate training and development

Professionals with a background in corporate training and development can make a smooth transition to Talent Acquisition Manager roles. Their expertise in assessing and enhancing employee skills aligns well with the talent acquisition process.

What is a talent acquisition manager?

A talent acquisition manager is a professional responsible for overseeing the recruitment and hiring processes within an organization. They develop strategies to attract and retain top talent, collaborate with hiring teams, and manage recruitment initiatives. Their goal is to identify, attract, and onboard qualified candidates who align with the organization's needs and values.

What is the difference between HR and talent acquisition manager?

Human Resources (HR) is a broader field encompassing various aspects of employee management, including recruitment, benefits administration, employee relations, training, and more. A talent acquisition manager specifically focuses on the recruitment process, from sourcing candidates to onboarding, and plays a strategic role in identifying and attracting the right candidates for the organization.

Should talent acquisition be part of HR?

Talent acquisition is often a subset of the HR function. While they share a common goal of managing human resources within an organization, Talent acquisition focuses exclusively on the recruitment process. Integrating talent acquisition within HR ensures alignment between recruitment efforts and the organization's overall HR strategy.

How much does a talent acquisition manager make?

The salary of a talent acquisition manager varies based on factors such as location, industry, level of experience, and company size. In the United States, the annual salary for a talent acquisition manager can range from $60,000 to $120,000 or more, depending on the organization and location. High-demand industries and competitive job markets may offer higher salaries.
