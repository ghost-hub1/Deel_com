How to become a Customer Experience Manager

Becoming a Customer Experience Manager typically involves a combination of education, practical experience, and the development of key skills focused on enhancing the overall customer journey. While there isn't a rigid, standardized path to becoming a Customer Experience Manager, several common steps can guide individuals aspiring to this role.

Common pathways to becoming a Customer Experience Manager

Cross-functional experience

Gaining experience in various areas of the business is valuable for a Customer Experience Manager. This might involve exposure to customer support, marketing, product development, and sales. A cross-functional perspective enables individuals to understand how different departments contribute to the overall customer experience.

Data analysis and interpretation

Customer Experience Managers should develop skills in data analysis to interpret customer feedback and metrics. Understanding customer data enables them to identify trends, areas for improvement, and opportunities to enhance the overall customer experience.

Networking

Building a professional network within the customer experience and related industries is valuable. Attending customer experience conferences, joining relevant associations, and connecting with professionals on platforms like LinkedIn can provide insights, share best practices, and offer collaboration opportunities.

Customer Experience Manager education and training

Education

A strong educational background in business, marketing, communications, or a related field is typically beneficial for individuals aspiring to become customer experience managers. Many customer experience managers hold a bachelor's or master's degree in areas such as:

  • Business administration
  • Marketing
  • Communications
  • Customer experience management
  • Hospitality management

Certifications

While not always mandatory, obtaining relevant certifications can enhance a customer experience manager's credibility and demonstrate expertise in customer experience management. Certifications include:

  • Customer Experience Professional (CCXP)
  • Certified Customer Experience Professional (CCEP)
  • Certified Customer Service Professional (CCSP)

Role-relevant skills

Beyond formal education and practical experience, there are several other fields aspiring Project Managers should gain a solid understanding of to excel in the role. These include:

  • Customer service and support
  • Customer journey mapping
  • Voice of the Customer (VoC) programs
  • Data analytics and metrics
  • Technology and customer experience platforms

Alternative career paths

In some cases, individuals transition into the role of Customer Experience Manager from different professional backgrounds. Understanding which roles align well with a transition to customer experience management can help plan a successful career switch.

Roles that fit well with a transition to Customer Experience Manager generally fall into these fields:

Customer service leadership

Professionals with experience in customer service leadership roles may transition into customer experience management, building on their expertise in managing customer interactions.

Marketing

Individuals with a background in marketing, particularly in areas focused on customer engagement and satisfaction, may transition into customer experience management.

Retail management

Professionals with a background in retail management, including store managers or district managers, can successfully transition into the role of a Customer Experience Manager. Their experience overseeing customer interactions, managing frontline staff, and ensuring a positive in-store experience aligns well with the customer-centric and service-oriented aspects of customer experience management. 

Hospitality

Professionals from a hospitality background may excel in implementing customer service initiatives, conducting staff training, and creating memorable customer experiences, all crucial to the success of a Customer Experience Manager.

Call center management

Professionals with a background in call center management, including call center supervisors or managers, can contribute valuable skills to the role of a Customer Experience Manager. Their experience in managing customer interactions, ensuring efficient call center operations, and optimizing service delivery aligns well with the customer-centric and process-oriented aspects of customer experience management.

What is a customer experience manager? 

A customer experience manager is a professional in charge of monitoring and optimizing every touchpoint a customer has with a company. Their goal is to ensure a positive and seamless experience throughout the customer journey, from initial interactions to post-purchase support. They focus on understanding customer needs, feedback, and preferences to enhance overall satisfaction and loyalty.

What are the KPIs for a customer experience manager? 

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for a customer experience manager may include:

    • Net Promoter Score (NPS): A customer loyalty and likelihood to recommend metric
    • Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Score: Gauges overall satisfaction with products or services
    • Customer Retention Rate: A metric measuring the percentage of customers retained over a period
    • Customer Effort Score (CES): Evaluates how easy it is for customers to interact with the company
    • First Response Time: Measures the speed of initial customer support responses
    • Churn Rate: Tracks the rate at which customers stop using a product or service

What is the difference between a customer experience manager and a customer success manager? 

A customer experience manager focuses on ensuring a positive overall experience across all interactions a customer has with a company. They work on improving processes, communication, and touchpoints throughout the customer journey. A customer success manager, on the other hand, specifically aims to maximize the value customers get from a company's offerings by guiding them toward success in using the products or services.

Is customer experience the same as customer service? 

Customer experience (CX) and customer service are related but distinct concepts. Customer experience covers every interaction a customer has with a company, including marketing, sales, support, and more. It considers the overall journey and impression left on the customer. Customer service, on the other hand, focuses specifically on resolving customer inquiries, issues, and providing support during and after the purchase. While both are essential, CX is broader and encompasses the entire customer journey.
